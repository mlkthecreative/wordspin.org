“Love not sleep lest thou come unto poverty”
Proverbs 20:13  ·  26th April, 2026  ·  MK.
“Love not sleep lest thou come unto poverty: Open thine eyes, and thou shall be satisfied with bread.”
I take this as literal and symbolic. We were made to work for our upkeep. In Literature classes (my favourite! How shocking!?) we were taught about outer meanings and inner meanings. Outer meaning here could be: if you love to sleep, then you will not eat, and you will fall into a desperate situation characterised by LACK. While this is true, it has bred a dangerous culture that frowns upon rest by linking it to laziness. This could be the reason why children in boarding schools (in parts of Africa) are not given the time to close their eyes and rest — because it is seen as a waste of time that could be used to do classwork or some sort of ‘show you are busy and focused’ activity. This ends up nurturing children who carry this mentality to their homes and children when they finally have them, causing a guilt or shame to develop in them for taking even a few minutes to themselves. Might I remind you that even God took a day to rest and appreciate his good creation in Genesis — in fact, after every day “he saw it was good.” By extension, he gave us a rest day. It is good to rest and it is good to appreciate the work that you have done. “PRACTICE GRATITUDE,” as therapists would say! Inner meaning here could be that we sleep on the things that matter and could feed us — give us bread. To me, this looks like not utilising the gifts and talents that have been given to us. How this links to ‘outer meaning’ is we work ourselves in a very linear way, or rather are made to move on a linear path that causes us NOT to see the benefit of using what comes so naturally to us (it is not entirely our fault!). I think this is part of the reason why we are encouraged to seek the Kingdom of God first, and then all these things shall be added to us. Why? Only he can PROPERLY help us / make us maintain the integrity of his design of us — from its goodness to its exponential growth potential being actualised: be fruitful and multiply. “Open thine eyes, and thou shall be satisfied with bread.”

21st May, 2026  ·  MK.
Hearing Aba explain the timeline of events unfolding before our eyes had me questioning his sensibility.
How does a low sperm count translate to impotence? He seemed to think he was impotent. How did he sleep with (seemingly) every girl he went on a date with? Why was he so fast? So many questionable decisions from a man I always found quite levelled in his judgement.
As has been the trend lately, almost every time I judge a person, I am humbled at the speed of light!
The last time I was deeply hurt, I started to reopen a dangerous chapter of my life. I stepped foot on ‘F*** Around and Find Out’ road and took a few steps forward. I am just blessed to have had God to KEEP ME FROM MYSELF and the danger I was allowing back in because I was in pain and didn’t even know it. I had even convinced myself that I could conquer the consequences. They couldn’t be that bad, right?
Before that though, years ago, when I experienced my first heartbreak from a person I thought I would marry, I went down the path I mention above. I was hurt, I couldn’t take any man seriously, but I still wanted a connection that I could only get from them. So, casual dating it was, with a heavy emphasis on serial — ***CRUNCH CRUNCH.
What I learned is I was NOT built for that life. It was too demanding and a cloud of guilt would keep me up because I was leading on people who did not deserve to be led on (not that anybody deserves to!). This is what weighed me down the most: I had potentially turned into a predator — one who preyed on people’s weaknesses for my very selfish desires/needs. In this case, I went for those who like-liked me and kept them for their bodies. That is all I wanted, all I pursued and all I thought men were good for. Their want for more value in us was irrelevant to me.
I quickly learned that I was not very good at “being bad”. I was forcing a character that was so out of character because I was hurt by someone I had allowed in and ended up being played for a fool. I did not want to experience that again so the easiest thing was to make sure I always had the upper hand in situation(ship)s.
Now, the point here isn’t for me to dwell on the nitty gritty stuff, but to say that heartbreak does a number on a person, especially when it pertains to something so deeply meaningful to them. In Aba’s case, he did not want to be shut out of his relationship, especially since his girlfriend was pregnant. His biggest fear as I understood it was being in a baby momma situation where they were separated and not a family. Having a child meant doing it the right way for the sake of the child. She did exactly what he asked her NOT TO and that hurt him deeply. For the first time, I got insight to what an abortion feels like for a man who is supportive and wanting of the child — not afraid, not ashamed and not even quiet about his feelings, but respectful of what the woman wants.
In my case, I had given a man a chance, which was not easy for me though we had the benefit of time and friendship. I wouldn’t call it sudden because it grew over years. He grew on me, he made sense to me. I am one of those unfortunate cases of sexual abuse as a child so you can probably imagine that my perception and relationship with boys and men wasn’t exactly trusting. It meant that a lot was worked and pushed through when it came to him. He was just different and I was willing to do the scary work to make it work.
We were off and on for what I can only blame on fear for years. I think we were both pretty good at sabotaging a good thing, but incapable of letting go. We were immature. I always remember my grandfather’s speech at my grandmother’s burial ceremony about “mature love” — we did not have what he described he had with my grandmother but I liked to think we could achieve it.
Fast forward and we finally seemed to align. I thought that finally, we were making our way into something mature, something brave that went through the storms. He was ready to talk about the things he usually ran from, he even initiated the conversations to much of my surprise. It wasn’t about “fun” or not going “too deep” even though it was THAT DEEP, it was about him wanting to understand love and how to love me because he loved me. He showed me a side of him that made me question whether I was dealing with the same guy because how and why?!
Don’t get me wrong, he had always been sensitive, he just avoided the sensitivity because it made him feel weak. But there he was being very vulnerable with me, without backing down, without even making his feelings and intentions less than what they were. There he was winning me back after an off time where I had got into settling on our relationship never making past a certain point.
I don’t know what your take on this is so far but contrary to what I think the sexual abuse fact may have painted in your mind, I could tell that our problem was the fear of getting too close and getting hurt because of it. I could sympathise with him, and knowing everything else, could hope because our friendship had shown me he was worth the try. He wasn’t an ass, he didn’t treat me poorly, he never used me, he was just afraid, just like I was afraid.
My presumption is that his shift was one last attempt at knowing what that life would be like with me since we had talked about it, just never been brave enough to live when the window of opportunity was ‘opportuning’. He had to come to terms with living a life he had not planned for while simultaneously letting go of the one he hoped for/dreamed about. He was a father and only told me a few days after the baby was born. I was crushed.
Following that, I wanted nothing serious to do with men. Why? You let them in and they let you down. Obviously that didn’t help the bigger male wound that I had, it was like reaggravating a healing wound by cutting it open again, with all its sensitive tissue. That’s way worse than before. That’s too much!
It’s one thing to be forced into something, you can argue and say that you never signed up for it — you had no say, you had no choice. It’s another thing to know that it is what you chose that hurt you: regret. I hated that I couldn’t hate him, and that all the tears I cried were because I loved him and he betrayed me. Everything we had turned into a lie yet I could not hate him. In my heart, in my mind, I said that no man would ever get me in that position again.
It was easy to judge Aba and label him ‘reckless’ and not what he portrays in the public sphere. Having raw sex with every woman he connected with after his ex, and fast, getting “everybody” pregnant. But, like I said, I was humbled at the speed of light(!) and reminded of what I did after a relationship with a man that hurt me deeply. How quick I was to move on to the next man, I guess to regain my dignity, power, strength…
Back to back and in rotation I went just like Aba, the only difference between us is I am a woman and I didn’t get pregnant. That’s it.
Just like that, I was SAT! The quickness, the unprotectedness, the serial mess! I lived it too.
I see that judgement only comes where there is self righteousness — where there is pride. I have been comfortable in my abstinent bubble, free of so much chaos, clear in ways I didn’t imagine possible. The danger of being in a bubble is being sheltered from the realities of the world and people, thinking they should be just like me as I am right now — stable (I have been stabilised).
The Late Pastor Voddie Baucham has a sermon on brokenness that is probably my favourite of all because I relate to it the most. We wish we could forget the things we have done that hurt us or hurt other people that we love. If we forgot though, we’d constantly make the same mistakes, we’d go back to that flame that hurt us and others. We would never learn, we would never grow. Memory preserves us.
▶ Pst. Voddie Baucham — On Brokenness
From my understanding and learning today, this is not about punishing self. It is not about living in anxiety or depression. It is merely about reminding us that when we think we’re “better than that”, we’re not. By the grace of God we are protected, helped and guided, but we are no better than the next person. We are kept humble through our memories and furthermore, are reminded why we NEED God, why we seek Him.
Somewhere up there I talk about getting hurt and stepping onto a ‘F*** around and find out’ path again. I was ready to throw all the beautiful progress I had made away because something (which I am not ready to talk about yet) that meant so much to me, dare I say everything to me, was trampled on. I had given all I had but it was not enough and I was completely drained. Just like that, I switched into a destructive mode. That taught me that I was (still) fickle. What saved me was having God in my heart/in my ear to pull me away from what I was doing and about to do which would certainly bring me great pain (and not just me but the ones I cared about too). This is why I say, it is GRACE, it is God’s LOVE that covers these flaws and imperfections, making sure we are empowered to make the right choices. Not the better choice, not the lesser evil, but the RIGHT choice.
Being reminded about that chapter in my life made me compassionate towards Aba. It’s not about excusing bad or evil behaviour, it’s about love that covers over a multitude of sins (1 Peter 4:8). Knowing your place, as uncomfortable as that may be, makes it possible to embrace the one who comes to or is brought to you with challenge/problem/sin, and just like God has empowered you, you are able to uplift them with love that only comes from His Holy Spirit — it is of the fruit, is it not?
